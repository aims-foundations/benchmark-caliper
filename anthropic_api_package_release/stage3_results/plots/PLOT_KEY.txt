================================================================================
PLOT KEY — Stage 3 Expert Validation Results
================================================================================

This file documents exactly what survey question or data source underlies
each element of each plot. All Likert items use a 1–5 scale:
  1 — Strongly disagree
  2 — Disagree
  3 — Neutral
  4 — Agree
  5 — Strongly agree

Experts reviewed automated validity assessments of benchmarks for specific
deployment contexts. Each expert reviewed 4 tuples (benchmark × deployment
context pairs) and 2 comparative benchmark pairs.


================================================================================
Plot 1: Likert Heatmap (1_likert_heatmap)
================================================================================

Per-expert mean across their 4 tuples for each column. Color scale 1–5.

COLUMNS (left to right):

  Score Appropriate
    Mean of 6 per-dimension Likert responses to:
    "The score of {X}/5 ({rating}) for {dimension} is appropriate for
     this deployment context."

  Justification Adequate
    Mean of 6 per-dimension Likert responses to:
    "The justification adequately explains why this score was assigned."

  Evidence Support
    Mean of 6 per-dimension Likert responses to:
    "The arguments for this dimension are well-supported by the cited
     evidence."

  Checklist Complete
    Mean of 6 per-dimension Likert responses to:
    "The checklist responses and information gaps capture the most
     important validity concerns for this dimension."

  Context (Summary)
    Mean of 4 Likert responses from the context/overall assessment page:
    1. "The overall summary paragraph accurately synthesizes the findings
        across all six dimensions."
    2. "The overall summary captures the most important validity concerns
        for this deployment context."
    3. "The 'Construct Depth' description accurately characterizes what
        this benchmark measures and does not measure."
    4. "The 'What Else You Need' recommendations would help a practitioner
        plan supplementary evaluation."

  Usefulness & Actionability
    Mean of 4 Likert responses from the usefulness page:
    1. "I would trust this assessment to inform a real deployment decision
        about this benchmark."
    2. "This assessment could help evaluation/benchmark design decisions
        for the deployment use case and target population of interest."
    3. "The remediation recommendations are reasonable."
    4. "The remediation recommendations are actionable."

ROWS:
  One per expert, ordered by descending mean Likert. Labels use anonymous
  codes with region abbreviations:
    E1 (FR/LU) = France / Luxembourg
    E2 (ET)    = Ethiopia
    E3 (EC/AR) = Ecuador / Argentina
    E4 (IN)    = India
    E5 (BD)    = Bangladesh


================================================================================
Plot 1b: Grand Mean Likert Bar (1b_likert_grand_mean)
================================================================================

Same 6 columns as Plot 1, but averaged across ALL 20 tuples (all experts
pooled). Error bars show standard deviation. Each bar aggregates the same
per-tuple means described above, then averages across tuples.


================================================================================
Plot 2: Risk Agreement (2_risk_agreement)
================================================================================

Stacked bar per expert showing distribution of responses to:

  "The pipeline assigned an overall risk rating of {RISK} for using
   {BENCHMARK} in this deployment context. How does this compare to
   your expert judgment?"

Response options (one per tuple, 4 per expert):
  - Too conservative (risk is lower)
  - About right
  - Too lenient (risk is higher)
  - Cannot assess


================================================================================
Plot 3: Novel Insights (3_novel_insights)
================================================================================

Aggregated bar chart (N=20 tuples) of responses to:

  "Did the assessment surface validity concerns you hadn't previously
   considered?"

Response options:
  - Yes — several new concerns
  - Yes — one or two
  - No — but it confirmed my existing concerns
  - No — it missed concerns I already knew about

Plot 4: Value vs. Expert Review (4_value_vs_expert)

Response options:

  - Useful complement to expert review

================================================================================
Plot 5: Confidence Calibration (5_confidence_calibration)
================================================================================

Grouped bar chart with one group per validity dimension (IO, IC, IF,
OO, OC, OF). Each group shows the count of responses (N=20 per dimension)
to a dimension-specific question:

  "The pipeline assigned {confidence level} confidence for this dimension.
   How does this compare to your judgment of the evidence strength?"

Response options:
  - Overconfident
  - About right
  - Underconfident
  - Cannot assess

The {confidence level} shown to the expert was the pipeline's own
confidence assessment for that specific dimension.


================================================================================
Plot 6: Comparative Ordering + Room to Grow (6_comparative_ordering)
================================================================================

LEFT PANEL — Ordering Agreement

Grouped bar chart showing expert agreement with the pipeline's relative
scoring of regional vs. global comparator benchmarks. Each comparative
pair compares a regional benchmark (proposed by the expert) against a
global standard benchmark (selected by the research team).

"Overall" group: responses to:
  "The pipeline scored {REGIONAL} at an average of {X}/5 and {COMPARATOR}
   at {Y}/5 for this deployment context. Does this relative ordering
   match your expert judgment?"

Per-dimension groups (IO–OF): responses to:
  "For {dimension}, the pipeline scored {REGIONAL} at {X}/5 and
   {COMPARATOR} at {Y}/5. Does this relative ordering match your
   judgment?"

Response options (same for all):
  - {REGIONAL} should score higher than {COMPARATOR}
  - Relative ordering is about right
  - {COMPARATOR} should score higher than {REGIONAL}
  - Scores should be approximately equal
  - Cannot assess

N=10 comparative pairs (5 experts × 2 pairs each).

RIGHT PANEL — Score Headroom (Room to Grow)

Single bar showing the mean (with std dev) of responses to:

  "The scoring leaves room to grow: a benchmark designed to address
   the gaps identified in both assessments could reasonably achieve a
   higher score for this deployment context."

Likert 1–5. N=10 comparative pairs.


================================================================================
Plot 7: Regional vs. Comparator Pipeline Scores (7_regional_vs_comparator)
================================================================================

NOT from expert survey responses. This plot shows the pipeline's own
automated validity scores (1–5 per dimension), comparing:

  Blue bars:   Regional benchmarks (N tuples, from primary_assessments.json)
  Orange bars: Global comparator benchmarks (N tuples, from scoring.json
               files in assessments/expert_*__<comparator>/ directories)

Each bar is the mean pipeline score for that dimension across all tuples
of that category. Error bars show standard deviation. Grand means for
each category are annotated at the bottom.

Regional benchmarks are culturally- and linguistically-specific benchmarks
proposed by experts for their deployment contexts. Global comparator
benchmarks (MMLU, GLUE, BLURB, etc.) are widely-used English-centric
benchmarks selected by the research team as reference standards.

The pipeline is expected to assign LOWER scores to global comparators
for these deployment contexts, since they lack cultural/linguistic
specificity. This plot verifies that discriminative behavior.

Score scale (per dimension):
  1 — Serious concern
  2 — Significant gaps
  3 — Moderate gaps
  4 — Minor gaps
  5 — Strong alignment


================================================================================
General Notes
================================================================================

EXPERTS:
  6 experts were recruited; 5 submitted responses (1 pending).
  Each expert reviewed 2 regional benchmarks × 2 deployment contexts
  = 4 primary tuples, plus 2 comparative benchmark pairs.

DIMENSIONS:
  IO = Input Ontology    OO = Output Ontology
  IC = Input Content     OC = Output Content
  IF = Input Form        OF = Output Form

  These are the six validity dimensions of the assessment framework.
  Each dimension evaluates a different aspect of benchmark-deployment
  alignment.

TUPLES:
  A "tuple" is a (benchmark, deployment context) pair — e.g., MILU
  evaluated for "Hindi-Medium Competitive Exam Aspirants in North India."
  Each tuple receives a full 6-dimension validity assessment from the
  pipeline.
